# UK Electricity Decarbonisation Analysis (2000–2025)

## Overview

This project explores the decarbonisation of the United Kingdom’s electricity system between 2000 and 2025 using data visualization and exploratory data analysis in R.

Using the Our World in Data Energy dataset, the analysis investigates how the UK transitioned from fossil fuel–dominated electricity generation toward low-carbon energy sources, and how this transition impacted carbon intensity over time.

The project focuses on communicating complex energy transition trends through clear, accessible, and data-driven storytelling.

---

## Key Research Questions

1. How has the share of electricity generated from fossil fuels changed between 2000 and 2025?
2. How have low-carbon electricity sources evolved over time?
3. How has the carbon intensity of electricity changed during the transition toward net zero?

---

## Key Insights

* Coal share collapsed from approximately 30% to under 1% by 2025, with the decline accelerating after the introduction of the UK Carbon Price Floor in 2013.
* Low-carbon electricity overtook fossil fuels around 2017.
* Wind became the dominant driver of low-carbon electricity growth.
* Carbon intensity fell from 522 gCO₂/kWh to 217 gCO₂/kWh, highlighting a major structural shift toward decarbonisation.

## Motivation

One of the main goals of this project was to use data visualization and storytelling to communicate structural changes in the UK energy system in a clear and accessible way.

The project combines analytical reasoning with visual communication to explore how policy, renewable energy growth, and fossil fuel decline have contributed to the UK’s progress toward net zero.
